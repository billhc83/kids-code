💡 LED (Light)

A tiny light bulb.

It has two legs:

Long leg = PLUS (+)

Short leg = MINUS (–)

A tiny traffic police officer 🚓 for electricity.

It slows electricity down so the LED does not break.

The little lines of color on the resistor tell you how strong it is!

Always use one with LEDs.

🧱 How a breadboard works (kid version)

Think of the breadboard like a LEGO city for wires 🏙️

Inside are hidden metal clips that grab the legs of parts.

🧠 Three important rules

Rule 1 – Rows are friends 🤝

If you plug two legs in the same row, they are holding hands.

Rule 2 – The middle gap is a river 🌊

Electricity cannot jump across the middle.

So parts like LEDs and buttons usually sit across the river.

Rule 3 – The rows on the long edges are connected

The long lines on the sides are electricity highways:

Red line = PLUS (+)

Blue line = MINUS (– / GND)

🧪 How we build a circuit

Put parts in different rows

Use wires to connect rows

Make a loop so electricity can go out and back home

A circle path = a circuit 🔁

How all the holes are connected on a breadboard

⚡ Method 1 — KidsCode Link Recommended

KidsCode Link is a small helper app that connects this website directly to your Arduino. No Arduino IDE needed — just click Upload in your lesson and your code goes straight to the board.

Step 1 — Download & Install

1

Visit the KidsCode Link download page and click ⬇ Download KidsCode Link. Requires Chrome or Edge browser on Windows 10 or 11.

2

Run the installer. Windows may show a security warning — click More info then Run anyway. This is normal for new apps.

3

Click through the installer (Next → Install → Finish). When it's done you'll see the KidsCode Link icon appear in the system tray — the bottom-right corner of your screen.

Step 2 — Connect your Arduino

4

Plug your Arduino into your computer using a USB-A to USB-B cable — that's the one with the square plug on the Arduino end.

5

Open your lesson in KidsCode. Look at the top bar — you should see ● KidsCode Link in green. If it shows in red, click it and follow the prompts.

Step 3 — Upload your code

6

Use the port dropdown at the top left to select your Arduino. It usually shows up as something like Arduino UNO (COM3). If nothing appears, click Refresh.

7

Compile checks your code for mistakes without sending it to the board. Upload compiles and sends it — your circuit will respond straight away!

8

Click Show Monitor to open the Serial Monitor — this shows messages printed by your Arduino, great for seeing what your code is doing.

🔧 If something isn't working

  • Board not in the port list — unplug the USB cable, plug it back in, then click Refresh.
  • KidsCode Link shows offline — check your system tray and open it from there, or click the red status indicator.
  • Upload fails — make sure the correct port is selected and no other program (like Arduino IDE) is using it.

🛠️ Method 2 — Arduino IDE
